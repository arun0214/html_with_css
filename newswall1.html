A New Way to Think About Conflicts of Interest in Medicine

Most of what we know about prescription drugs and medical devices comes from industry-funded clinical trials. Does the source of funding affect study findings?The question is at the heart of a long standing debate about financial conflicts ofinterest in medicine and what to do about them. That debate was recently reinvigorated by a three-part series of articles on the subject in The New England Journal of Medicine. For many years that journal has, as have other medical journals, required study authors to disclose such conflicts.
